Your Dashboard

Learn how to make the most of your CPNW dashboard. This walkthrough highlights every panel, icon, and notification so you can keep compliance tasks on track and stay aware of upcoming deadlines.

Look to the left side of the dashboard for the My Records tile. Selecting it reveals a compact menu so you can jump directly to the documentation you use most. The links open in the same view, letting you stay oriented while you upload files or confirm statuses.

  • Requirements — Review immunizations, eLearning modules, CPR cards, and any program- or facility-specific items. Each entry shows status, due dates, and upload controls for new documents.
  • eLearning Results — See completion dates, scores (when applicable), and download PDF proof of every module directly from this page.
  • OIG/SAM Check — Track the automated exclusion scan CPNW runs on the 1st and 15th of each month.

My Account menu

Directly below My Records you will see My Account. Selecting it expands a second menu that focuses on personal settings and profile updates:

  • My Profile — Update your name, primary contact phone/email, emergency contact details, and mailing address. Save changes to keep coordinators informed.
  • My Security Settings — Change your password and choose how you want to receive two-factor authentication codes (email or text message).
  • My Demographics — Upload a profile photo.

Notifications

On the right side of the dashboard, the Notifications panel keeps you informed without leaving the page. Each card highlights time-sensitive items:

  • Alerts when new requirements are posted for your program or facility.
  • Messages sent by coordinators or document reviewers, including follow-up questions.
  • Status updates when pending requirements flip to approved or need a correction.
  • Reminders as annual requirements approach expiration so you have time to renew.

Clinical assignments

At the bottom of the dashboard you'll find the clinical assignments table. Each row shows:

  • The healthcare facility you are assigned to.
  • Rotation start and end dates for quick reference.
  • The assignment status (approved or rejected). A rejected status simply indicates an outstanding issue and will flip to approved once the issue is resolved.
