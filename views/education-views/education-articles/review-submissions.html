Educational Coordinator Guide

Review Submissions

Use the Review Submissions table to monitor incoming requirements, open each student record, and record clear decisions.

Open the Review page

  1. From the Dashboard, expand Admin and click Review.
  2. The Review Submissions table lists each student with columns for Name, Verified, Status, Program, School, SID/EID, and View.
  3. Students with at least one Educational or CPNW requirement in Submitted or In Review appear first with a Needs Review status.

Inspect a student’s record

Click the student’s name or the View button to open the detail modal.

  • The modal shows school/program info, clinical assignments, and, in most service configurations, 21 CPNW requirements (10 eLearning modules + 11 immunization/other items).
  • eLearning module statuses update automaticallyâ€”no action required.
  • If your program uses CPNW’s Document Verification Specialist (DVS) services, CPNW reviewers handle requirement approval, though you can still inspect submissions. Programs without DVS must review and decide on each CPNW requirement themselves.

Review an individual requirement

  1. Scan the first two columns (Requirement Name + Status) and look for any listed as In Review.
  2. Click the requirement name to open its detail modal. You’ll see submission data (dates, locations, responses), optional declination reasons, and any uploaded documents.
  3. Choose Accept, Conditionally Approved (if available), or Reject, then click Save.
  4. If you select Conditionally Approved or Reject, enter a message explaining the condition or rejection reason. The message appears in the modal’s message pane and is sent to the student.

Communicate and follow up

  • Students receive a notification whenever the status changes. Remind them to open the requirement to read your comments and upload any fixes.
  • When a student resubmits, the status returns to Submitted and the table again lists the student as Needs Review.
  • For rejections tied to background results, coordinators can override by approving if an exception is warranted, or leave the rejection to block the clinical assignment.
